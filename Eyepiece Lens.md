# Eyepiece Lens
The Eyepiece lens, a convex lens placed near the user's eyes to manipulate light in such a way that the focal point is long. The distance between the focal point and the optical centre of the lens determines the clarity/focus of the image seen.
The shorter the distance, the closer the focus plane is relative to the user.
The radius of the lens is large, in order to maximise area of vision experienced by the user.

## Flexible Membrane

The flexible membrane (FMembrane) is made of transparent, flexible material. It can be a silicon sheet, but due to accesibility issues, Treated acrylic tape is used instead. The stickiness can be reduced by washing using soap and water.
The FMembrane is supported by cut bottle caps. A big hole is cut at the center of the bottle cap. The hole allows a spherical geometry when the membrane expands and bulges. The remainder is cut in such a way only a flat ring with shallow depth is left. 

## Assembly

1. A hot sewing needle is used to make two holes at the circumference of the flat ring such that a syringe needle can be inserted and exposed to the chamber of the lens. Needle shouldn't be wiggled around to prevent makign a hole too large for a snug fit of the syringe needle.
2. The flat ring is placed on the remainder of the bottle cap, as a raising platform to ease application of acrylic tape.
3. Acrylic tape is stretched then wrapped around the flat ring. Excess tape hanging off the sides is cut off. 
4. The flat ring-membrane composition is put on the table with membrane resting in a puddle of water on the table. Alternatively, the composition can be rested on the remainder of the bottle cap, as a raising platform to ease application of acrylic tape.
5. Acrylic tape is stretched then wrapped around the flat ring. Excess tape hanging off the sides is cut off.
6. CA glue is applied to where the acrylic tapes meet. Be careful not to let CA glue reach any optically important areas like the transparent area as it will cause permanent fogginess in the lens and obscure the user's vision through the lens. The whole process of preparation and assembly have to be repeated if fogginess happens.
7. A syringe needle is inserted into one of the holes with the needle tip positioned near the inner walls of the flat ring. This allows for easy air bubble removal.
8. Once in position, CA glue is applied at the exterior of the intersection between the ring and the needle. Needle have to be held in place until glue fully cures. After 5-10 minutes, sprinkling of baking soda can be performed to speed up the cure and create a rigid mass to hold the needle firmly in place. CA glue acts as both sealant and adhesive, to prevent water leakage and to hold the composition together.
9. Another syringe needle is inserted into the other hole in the same fashion as (step 7), for the same reason. CA glue is applied in the same way as (step 8).
10. A 5ml syringe filled with water is attached to one of the needles and pumped to introduce water into the lens. Air pocket can be sucked away at this stage too. Repeat until lens buldges with water without any air bubbles.
11. The same 5ml syringe is inserted fully-filled with water. Any air bubbles in the needle or syringe should be removed before attaching the two parts.
12. (Step 11) is repeated for the other needle with a half-filled 1ml syringe. This syringe is for fine focus control, while the 5ml syringe is for quick, coarse focus control.

## Test 
1. Put pressure on the coarse focus syringe and check for leaks. If there is any, release the pressure and apply CA glue to the spot of leak to seal the hole.
